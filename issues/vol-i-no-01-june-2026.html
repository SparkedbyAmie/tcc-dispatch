From the Founders

A room that finally exists.

May was our first real month — and you showed us exactly what we'd been dreaming of.

When we started The Continental Collective, we kept describing a feeling more than a thing: a room where grown, diasporic folks could exhale, be curious, and really be present with one another. In May, that room stopped being an idea. It opened its doors. We took our first Perfume & PJs public, kicked off our very first Member Moment, and watched the community we'd been imagining show up in real bodies, in real conversation, on real evenings.

This is our first Dispatch — a little monthly letter home to the people building this with us. We'll always look back at the month that just closed, then point you toward what's next. Thank you for being early. It means everything.

In the Room

Perfume & PJs, in public for the first time.

We took Perfume & PJs out of the testing phase and into the world — our first public edition, hosted at Industrious in Carmel. It was an evening of intentional conversation and genuinely good vibes: soft lighting, softer guards, and the kind of honesty that only happens when women feel safe enough to be themselves.

To the three women who showed up and chose themselves for a girls' night — thank you. You didn't just attend an event. You set the tone for every room that comes after this one.

Member Moments, Launched

Walk, Stretch, Dialogue.

Our very first Member Moment — and the start of something we've wanted for a while.

On Memorial Day morning, Adam gathered the crew at Founders Park for the first-ever Walk, Stretch, Dialogue. Member Moments are exactly what they sound like: a chance to be together outside our regular programming, low stakes, no pressure — just movement, fresh air, and honest conversation. We move, we stretch, we talk. That's the whole thing. And it was perfect.

Founding Member — No. 001
Member Spotlight

Meet Ayo.

Our very first Founding Member — and one of the men in the room when it all began.

Every community has a first. For us, that's Ayo. He didn't wait for the proof, the track record, or the crowd — he believed in the room before there was a room, and put his name down as our inaugural Founding Member. He was also there for the very first Men's Collective back in April, in the space Adam built for men to gather with intention.

We asked Ayo a few questions. His answers were short, specific, and exactly the kind we hope every spotlight feels like.

where it began ✦

Where are you from?
Indianapolis, IN.

What made you say yes to the Collective?
Loved the idea of a men's forum.

What song is playing on repeat right now?
"Waltz in 7/8" by Yanni.

Sunday morning — how do you actually spend it?
Church.

What's your reset?
A drive — especially to the countryside.

You were our first Founding Member. You put your name down before there was a track record, before there was proof. What did you see?
I like the idea of forward-oriented young men meeting to socialize and discuss.

The right room is one where ___.
You are your authentic self.

Ayo, thank you for going first. The whole thing is steadier because you did.

"The right room is one where you are your authentic self." — Ayo, Founding Member No. 001
